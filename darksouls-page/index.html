In a dying world where the First Flame fades, the cycle of fire and dark nears its end. To prevent the coming of the Age of Dark, ancient heroes known as the Lords of Cinder—beings who once linked the flame—must return to their thrones and rekindle the fire once more. You are the Ashen One, an undead risen from the grave, not to link the flame, but to find your place in a world collapsing under the weight of countless cycles. As you hunt down the fallen Lords—such as Aldrich, Yhorm, and Lothric—you uncover the truth: the flame is no longer worth kindling, and the age of fire is decaying beyond repair. Ultimately, you face the decision to link the flame, let it fade, or steal its power—a final act in an endless cycle of fire, sacrifice, and rebirth

Main Bosses

Ludleth of Courland is a Lord of Cinder who willingly linked the fire. He remains seated in the shrine, offering soul transposition and speaking quietly of the cost of his sacrifice.

The Abyss Watchers are undead warriors who became Lords of Cinder. Once sworn to fight the Abyss, they were eventually consumed by it and turned against each other in endless combat.

Aldrich, the Devourer of Gods, is a former cleric who became a Lord of Cinder after consuming gods. He now resides in Anor Londo, a grotesque figure embodying gluttony and darkness.

Yhorm the Giant is a Lord of Cinder and former ruler of Stormdawn Castle. He sacrificed himself to link the fire, driven by a sense of honor and a tragic connection to his people.

Lorian and Lothric are brothers and Lords of Cinder. Lorian is a knight bound to a wheelchair, while Lothric refused to link the fire, leading to their tragic fate.

The Soul of Cinder is the final boss of Dark Souls III, representing all the Lords of Cinder who linked the fire. It embodies the flame’s essence and marks the end of the Age of Fire.
